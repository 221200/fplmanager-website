How does it work?

All of the analysis methods on this website utilise Linear Programming (LP) techniques to allow you to optimise your team.

Selecting the optimal fantasy football team is a special version of the famous Knapsack Problem. The Knapsack Problem can be stated in the following way:

Suppose you are a burglar who has a bag with a limited carrying capacity. You break into an electronics store and want to maximise the value of the items that you steal, without exceeding your bag's carrying capacity. Some items in the store are small and expensive (mobile phones), some are small and inexpensive (CDs), some items are large and expensive (flat screen televisions), and some items are large and inexpensive (computer desks). Which items do you take to maximise your profits from this particular heist?

This problem can be reworded to apply to the selection of a football team:

Suppose you are a football manager who has a team with a limited budget. You want to maximise the quality of the players that you select, without exceeding your team's budget. Some players in the league are cheap and high quality, some are cheap and low-quality, some players are expensive and high quality, whilst some players are expensive and low-quality. Which players do you select to maximise your team's return from this particular gameweek?

There are many ways to solve these types of problems. Rather than reinventing the wheel and developing a bespoke LP solver, a Python library called PuLP has been used. PuLP is an open-source Linear Programming library and comes packaged with lightning fast commercial-grade solvers - simply plug in your constraints and PuLP will take care of the rest.
